Helicopter Noise Legislation:
Don't Throw Your Earplugs Away
... Yet

A lot of noise -and self-congratulation between local,
state, and federal legislators ...for nothing in view.

Almost six months have passed since it was announced all over the local news that new bills were on their ways to limit helicopters traffic -and noise- in NYC to essential needs and to crack down on private companies. Bills on this topic have been proposed at the city, state, and federal levels. None of them are even close to be voted as laws.

Manhattan CB6 ranks 4th in the number of helicopter noise complaints. However, the heliport at 34th East has already been limited to essential flights 5 years ago (and no flight on the weekend) so little change should be expected there.

The Heliport is closed on weekends. From Monday through Friday, operations are allowed from 8AM to 8PM.
About 28,000 max -only essential- flights per year are allowed.
The operator of the heliport is responsible for the good maintenance of the buildings and environment.

So far, in 2022, there were 18,283 complaints related to helicopters.
In 2021, there were 25,916 complaints.
In 2020, 10,358 complaints.
And in 2019, 25,916 complaints.
These numbers show no fluctuation if we consider that the drop in 2020 was due to the pandemic.

CB6 Chairperson Kyle Athayde would love to see this heliport closed and have it redeveloped for the local people.

Right now, it looks like a gated waste disposal.
